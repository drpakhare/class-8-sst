Section B Short Answer

Q11
Human resource = people who apply knowledge, skills and decision-making to create goods/services. Examples: a scientist, chef, doctor, teacher, police officer (any two).
Q12
Land → rent; Labour → wages; Capital → interest; Entrepreneurship → profit.
Q13
Labour = the physical & mental effort used in production. Human capital = the specialised skills, knowledge and expertise (the quality) behind that effort.

Section C Long Answer

Q14
Human capital = knowledge, skills, experience & abilities of people (built by education, training, health); embodied in people. Physical capital = human-made assets (machinery, tools, buildings) and money; can be bought/sold/transferred.
Q15
(1) Education & training — literacy to expertise, plus hands-on practice; (2) Healthcare — good health improves learning & work; (3) Social/cultural influences — work ethic like Japan's kaizen or the German focus on quality.
Q16
(1) Personal savings, family & friends (first source); (2) Bank loan — repaid with interest; (3) Stock market — big firms sell shares to the public and pay dividends.

Section D Very Long Answer

Q17
Entrepreneur = a person who has an idea, takes risks, gathers factors of production and works to make a startup succeed. Five tasks: identifies a problem & is resolute to solve it innovatively; takes risks (money & time); combines the factors of production; makes key operating decisions; contributes to society's welfare (jobs, livelihoods).
OR — Responsibilities: to natural resources — use responsibly, reduce waste, avoid pollution (e.g. leather-factory waste, e-waste with lead/mercury) for future generations; to workers — fair pay & safe conditions, skill development, workplace rights. CSR: India was the first country (2014) to mandate spending 2% of average 3-year profit on social/environmental good.
