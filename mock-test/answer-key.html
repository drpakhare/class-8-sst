Section C Long Answer

Q14
Meet present needs without harming future generations. (1) Restore & regenerate renewables within recovery limits; (2) use non-renewables judiciously till greener options come; (3) ensure fair access to basic resources for all.
Q15
Human capital = people's knowledge/skills/experience (built by education, training, health), embodied in people. Physical capital = human-made assets (machinery, tools, buildings) & money that can be bought/sold.
Q16
(1) Shifting the capital to Daulatabad — people forced to travel 1,000+ km, then moved back → loss of life. (2) Token currency — copper coins declared equal to silver/gold → counterfeiting → economic decline.

Section D Very Long Answer

Q17
Akbar: early brutality (Chittor) → grew tolerant; abolished jizya, promoted sulh-i-kul, Rajput marriage alliances, Hindu officials, interfaith dialogues, house of translation → stable, expanded empire.
OR — Punjab: HYV wheat/paddy needed more water; free power → over-pumping; chemicals polluted groundwater → ~80% "over-exploited", water below ~30 m. Lesson: short-term food security caused long-term unsustainability; use within recovery limits.
Q18
Entrepreneur: has an idea, takes risks, gathers factors, makes a startup succeed. Five tasks: identify a problem & resolve to solve it; take risks (money/time); combine factors of production; make key decisions; contribute to society's welfare.
OR — Four factors: Land (natural resources, e.g. soil/water); Labour (physical & mental effort, e.g. a carpenter); Capital (money + machinery/tools); Entrepreneurship (risk-taking organiser who starts the business).
